Cause of the problem

Most of the empty nester families are because their children are always out of town and rarely come home, and the elderly live alone. There are many factors that have led to the problem, including both macro and micro perspectives.

From the microscopic point of view, because of the one-child policy implemented in China since the last century, most of the young adults nowadays are the only child of their families, so the pressure to support the elderly is high. Further, due to the fewer job opportunities in rural areas and small towns, most young people choose to go to the big cities to earn money, and can only return to their hometowns once or twice a year. In families with multiple children, several children may take turns caring for the elderly or one of the children may be primarily responsible for support, while in families with one child, it is difficult for them to accompany the elderly on a regular basis.

In addition, with the development of social and economic development, the traditional Chinese concept of “a family living under a roof” is gradually fading out of people’s lives. Elderly people who are financially capable and in good health will choose to live alone by themselves so as not to disturb the lives of their children. Or others have deep sentiments about a certain place and simply want to spend their retirement life in the place, in this case they will also choose to live in the certain place and apart from their children.

To summarize, empty nesters may seem to be an internal problem of each family, but it is actually caused by the trend of the whole society and its policies.
